CHAPTER 1

Zhou Bao writhed and struggled in his bed. The sheets wound more and more tightly around his sweaty body until he felt like a giant snake was coiling around him, crushing the air out of his lungs. Suddenly his whole body stiffened; every muscle became tight as a knot. In his feverish mind, Bao dreamed of dazzling colours. They blinded him. Flashes of yellow, red and green stung his eyes and made him jerk and flinch in his sleep. They were like a multi-coloured mortar attack. Gasping for breath, he tried to run away. Careering through the narrow streets of Old Shanghai, his tired legs burning, he tried to outrun the exploding lights but, whichever way he turned,

there was a dead end ahead and, waiting for him, another blinding explosion of purple or blue. The searing heat scalded his skin. How he wished he could wake up from this nightmare.

He knew he was asleep and he knew he was dreaming, but somehow he couldn't shake off the images of overpowering light. All through the early hours of that humid August morning, rainbow colours tormented his mind.
